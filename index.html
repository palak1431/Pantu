Educational technology, also known as EduTech or EdTech,[1] combines the use of physical hardware, software, and educational theoretic to facilitate learning. Educational technology creates, uses, and manages technological processes and educational resources to help improve user academic performance.[2] The field has been described as a persisting initiative that seeks to bring learners, teacher, and technical means together in an effective way.[3]

In addition to experiential knowledge drawn from educational practice, educational technology is based on theoretical knowledge that emerge out of various disciplines such as communication, education, psychology, sociology, artificial intelligence, and computer science, among others.[1] It encompasses several domains including learning theory, computer-based training, online learning, and m-learning, where mobile technologies are used.[4]
